About REELS

"REELS" is an acronym that stands for "Recording of Events by Enthusiasts in Local Schools." The owner and founder of this non-profit organization, Natalie Hauge, spent her junior and senior year of high school in a digital entrepreneurship class at Heartland Technical Academy at Richland Community College in Decatur, Illinois and still utilizes to this day the knowledge and tactics in the audio-visual field that she had been educated in during that class. She then attended Millikin University and earned a degree in video production in their Arts and Technology department.

REELS's ultimate "missions" are these three things. One mission is to help high school and college students get into the career paths of the film and media world. Although the student volunteers are not paid with money, they're rewarded with new entries they can add to their resumes: professional work experience in the audio-visual field as well as the volunteer entry everyone needs. It would help them to get a job in the industry in the future. Another mission is to help schools and other businesses raise money through fundraisers.

The third mission is to improve upon the documentation of events and happenings in our state of Illinois. In the past, if they were taped at all, it was only with the use of a single, overview, far-away, low-quality shot that may be hard to see and sometimes wouldn't even play as a sold DVD copy.



Natalie created this organization upon her personal experience. Her sister used to dance at the Dance Centre in Decatur when she was younger, was a member of the Mount Zion Poms Squad, and was both a dancer and a dance teacher at Richland Community College in Decatur. Her family would always buy a DVD copy if the recitals were recorded. However, Richland wasn't always taped, and the Dance Centre DVDs would either malfunction, break, not play, only play certain dances or just one act, or just plain not work at all. Additionally, a Dance Centre performance was sometimes filmed with such a poor-quality camera that you could not even tell who was who in the video. And watching the Poms Squad on the football field, it got Natalie thinking how if the school had a big crew of camera workers to tape the event, then they'd be able to not only create videos of good quality and changing angles, but more importantly, the videos would be able to be edited to focus on just one individual student playing or dancing at the game. Each individual could then buy his or her own copy of the game, but a particular edited copy that would be just about him or her.
