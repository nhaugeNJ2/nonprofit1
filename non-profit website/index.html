REELS is a non-profit organization in Illinois that seeks to improve and encourage the proper capturing of local events strictly by the help of volunteers from high schools and colleges. We are always seeking more creative students to help document all of our state's greatest events!







Apply Now!




After finding a local event, announcements are made at local schools for open interviews with the organization. Once a student volunteer crew is assembled, the owners or hosts of the event will pay the organization to film it. That money will then be used to buy more cameras and equipment.

Depending on the type of event, each volunteer is assigned to tape a certain part of the event or certain individuals. Some volunteers will tape an overhead view, others faraway angles, and others closer shots to focus in on particular people, as examples. Some volunteers will also tape interviews with a microphone and others b-roll.

After filming is finished, other student volunteers will be assigned to edit specific videos, some with a focus on one particular person or one part of the event. For example, each and every dancer at a recital will be able to buy both a DVD copy of the whole show as well as an edited version of his or her own performances that just focuses on him or her specifically along with his or her own interview.

The money made from the selling of the DVDs would then work as a fundraiser for whatever place or company the event happened in, whether it was a school, a dance company, a theater company, a campaign, a church, a park, etc.
